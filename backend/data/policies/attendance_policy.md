# Attendance Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** June 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources
**Applies To:** All full-time and part-time employees

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes expectations for employee attendance, punctuality, and time tracking. It ensures consistent attendance standards while providing flexibility for remote work and personal needs. Reliable attendance is essential to maintaining team productivity and business operations.

---

## 2. Working Hours

### 2.1 Standard Schedule

- Standard working hours: **9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday** (including a 1-hour unpaid lunch break).
- Total working hours: **40 hours per week** for full-time employees.
- Part-time employees work the hours specified in their employment agreement.

### 2.2 Flexible Start Times

- Employees may select a flexible start time between **7:00 AM and 10:00 AM**.
- The selected start time must be approved by the employee's direct manager.
- Once set, the start time should remain consistent. Changes require 1 week's notice to the manager.
- The employee's core working hours remain the same regardless of start time.

### 2.3 Core Hours

- **Core hours: 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM.** All employees must be available and reachable during this period.
- Meetings, collaborative work, and team standups should be scheduled within core hours.
- Exceptions may be granted for employees in different time zones, with manager approval.

### 2.4 Overtime

- Overtime is defined as any hours worked beyond **40 hours per week**.
- Overtime must be **pre-approved** by the employee's direct manager.
- Non-exempt employees are paid overtime at **1.5 times their regular hourly rate**.
- Overtime exceeding 10 hours per week requires **VP-level approval**.
- Exempt employees do not receive overtime pay but may be eligible for compensatory time off at management discretion.

## 3. Remote Work

### 3.1 Hybrid Remote Work

- Employees may work remotely up to **3 days per week** with manager approval.
- Remote work requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least **1 week** in advance.
- Managers may deny or modify remote work requests based on:
  - Team needs and project deadlines.
  - Employee performance and attendance history.
  - Role requirements (e.g., roles requiring on-site equipment).
- Remote work is a privilege, not a right. It may be revoked based on performance or attendance issues.

### 3.2 Fully Remote Work

- Fully remote arrangements require **VP-level approval** and a formal remote work agreement.
- Eligibility criteria:
  - Minimum **12 months** of continuous employment.
  - Consistently meets or exceeds performance expectations.
  - Role is suitable for full-time remote work (determined by the manager and HR).
- Fully remote employees must:
  - Maintain reliable internet connectivity (minimum 50 Mbps download, 10 Mbps upload).
  - Be available during core hours (10:00 AM – 4:00 PM in their local time zone).
  - Attend in-person meetings or team events as required (travel costs covered by the company for up to 4 trips per year).
  - Maintain a dedicated, ergonomic workspace.

### 3.3 Remote Work Responsibilities

- **Employee responsibilities:**
  - Maintain a professional and distraction-free workspace.
  - Use company-approved devices and software.
  - Follow all data security and privacy policies.
  - Be available during core hours and respond to messages within **30 minutes**.
  - Keep manager informed of any changes to availability.
- **Manager responsibilities:**
  - Ensure remote employees are included in team communications and meetings.
  - Evaluate remote employees based on output and results, not hours worked.
  - Conduct regular check-ins (at least weekly) with remote team members.
  - Ensure equitable treatment of remote and on-site employees.

## 4. Time Tracking

### 4.1 Daily Time Logging

- All employees must log their hours worked **daily** using the company's time tracking system.
- Time entries must be accurate and submitted by **end of day**.
- Time entries cannot be modified after **2 business days** without manager approval.
- Failure to log time for 3 or more consecutive days may result in a written warning.

### 4.2 Time Correction Procedure

- If an employee needs to correct a time entry, they must:
  1. Submit a Time Correction Request through the HR portal.
  2. Provide a brief explanation for the correction.
  3. Obtain manager approval within **2 business days**.
- Time corrections exceeding **4 hours** in a single day require HR approval.

### 4.3 Overtime Tracking

- Non-exempt employees must log all overtime hours as they occur.
- Overtime is calculated weekly (Monday through Sunday).
- Managers receive weekly overtime reports and must review them for accuracy.

## 5. Absenteeism

### 5.1 Notification Requirements

- Employees must notify their manager **before their scheduled start time** if they will be absent or late.
- Notification should be made via phone call, text message, or the company's absence reporting system. Email alone is not sufficient.
- If the employee is unable to notify their manager, they must notify HR or a designated backup.
- For absences exceeding **3 consecutive days**, employees must contact HR directly in addition to their manager.

### 5.2 Unscheduled Absence Tracking

- Unscheduled absences are tracked on a rolling **12-month basis**.
- The following thresholds trigger progressive action:
  - **3 unscheduled absences in a quarter:** Verbal warning and attendance discussion with manager.
  - **5 unscheduled absences in a quarter:** Written warning and HR involvement.
  - **8 unscheduled absences in a rolling 12-month period:** Formal performance improvement plan (PIP) for attendance.
  - **10 or more unscheduled absences in a rolling 12-month period:** Disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

### 5.3 No-Call/No-Show

- A "no-call/no-show" occurs when an employee fails to report to work and fails to notify their manager or HR before their scheduled start time.
- **First occurrence:** Written warning.
- **Second occurrence within 12 months:** Final written warning.
- **Third occurrence within 12 months:** May be considered job abandonment and grounds for termination.
- A no-call/no-show of **3 or more consecutive business days** without communication is considered voluntary resignation (job abandonment).

### 5.4 Chronic Absenteeism

- Chronic absenteeism is defined as patterns of absence that significantly impact productivity and team operations, including:
  - Frequent Monday/Friday absences.
  - Absences immediately before or after holidays or vacations.
  - Recurring short-term absences that exceed the thresholds above.
- Chronic absenteeism may result in:
  1. Investigation by HR to determine if the absences are protected (e.g., FMLA, ADA).
  2. Fitness-for-duty evaluation if medical issues are cited.
  3. Disciplinary action if the absences are not protected.

## 6. Punctuality

### 6.1 Late Arrival

- Employees are expected to arrive at their scheduled start time.
- An employee is considered "late" if they arrive more than **5 minutes** after their scheduled start time.
- Late arrivals are tracked and may contribute to attendance action thresholds.
- Pattern tardiness (e.g., arriving late on the same day each week) is treated as an attendance issue.

### 6.2 Early Departure

- Employees may not leave before their scheduled end time without manager approval.
- Early departures for personal appointments must be requested in advance and are recorded as partial-day absences.
- Leaving early without approval on a regular basis may result in attendance warnings.

## 7. Special Attendance Situations

### 7.1 Jury Duty and Court Appearances

- Employees summoned for jury duty or required to appear in court receive **paid leave** for the duration of service, up to **10 business days** per calendar year.
- Beyond 10 days, leave is unpaid but the employee's position is guaranteed.
- Employees must provide a copy of the summons to HR and notify their manager as soon as possible.
- Employees may not attempt to avoid jury duty service.

### 7.2 Voting Leave

- Employees may take up to **2 hours of paid leave** on Election Day to vote, if polls are not open during non-working hours.
- Employees must notify their manager at least **3 business days** in advance.

### 7.3 Bereavement

- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): **5 days** paid leave.
- Extended family (grandparent, grandchild, in-law): **3 days** paid leave.
- Additional unpaid leave may be granted with manager approval.
- Documentation (death certificate or obituary) must be provided within 5 business days of return.

### 7.4 Military Leave

- Employees called to active duty or military service are entitled to leave under the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA).
- Military leave is unpaid (unless the employee elects to use accrued PTO).
- The employee's position is guaranteed upon return, per USERRA requirements.
- Employees must provide at least **30 days' notice** before deployment when possible.

### 7.5 Religious Observances

- The company provides reasonable accommodations for religious observances.
- Employees must notify HR at least **2 weeks** in advance of religious observances that require time off.
- Religious leave is unpaid unless the employee uses accrued PTO.

## 8. Attendance and Performance

- Attendance is a component of the annual performance review.
- Excessive unscheduled absences, even if individually excused, may impact performance ratings.
- Managers must document attendance issues and discuss them during regular check-ins.
- Attendance records are maintained in the HRIS and are accessible to the employee through the HR portal.

---

## 9. Accommodations for Disabilities

- The company provides reasonable accommodations for employees with disabilities that affect attendance, in accordance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).
- Accommodation requests must be made through HR and supported by medical documentation.
- Accommodations may include: modified work schedules, additional break time, remote work, or reduced hours.
- Each request is evaluated on a case-by-case basis through the interactive process.

---

## 10. Manager Responsibilities

- Managers must:
  - Track and document employee attendance accurately.
  - Address attendance issues promptly and consistently.
  - Apply attendance policies fairly across the team.
  - Maintain confidentiality regarding medical or personal leave.
  - Report extended absences to HR for tracking and support.
  - Ensure adequate coverage during employee absences.
- Managers must **not**:
  - Discourage employees from taking legitimate leave.
  - Retaliate against employees for filing attendance-related complaints or requests for accommodation.
  - Make assumptions about the reasons for absence without proper investigation.

## 11. Technology and Tools

- The company uses the following tools for time tracking and attendance management:
  - **HRIS Portal:** For time logging, absence requests, and PTO tracking.
  - **Absence Reporting System:** For real-time absence notifications.
  - **Time Clock:** For non-exempt employees requiring physical clock-in/clock-out.
- Employees are responsible for familiarizing themselves with these tools during onboarding.
- Technical issues with time tracking systems must be reported to IT within **24 hours**.

## 12. Policy Violations and Disciplinary Action

| Violation | First Offense | Second Offense | Third Offense |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| Late arrival (pattern) | Verbal warning | Written warning | Final warning |
| No-call/no-show | Written warning | Final warning | Termination |
| Unauthorized absence | Written warning | Final warning | Termination |
| Falsifying time records | Final warning | Termination | — |
| Chronic absenteeism | Attendance PIP | Final warning | Termination |

- Disciplinary actions are subject to the company's progressive discipline policy.
- Employees may appeal disciplinary actions through the Grievance Procedure outlined in the Employee Handbook.
