# Compliance Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** June 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Legal & Compliance Department
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, and third-party representatives

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the company's commitment to ethical business practices, legal compliance, and regulatory adherence. It provides guidance on expected conduct, reporting obligations, and consequences for non-compliance. All personnel must understand and adhere to these standards.

---

## 2. Code of Conduct

### 2.1 General Standards

- All employees must act with integrity, honesty, and professionalism in all business dealings.
- Employees must comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and company policies.
- Employees must not engage in any activity that creates a conflict of interest or the appearance of a conflict.
- The company maintains a **zero-tolerance policy** for fraud, theft, harassment, discrimination, and retaliation.

### 2.2 Conflicts of Interest

- Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest within **30 days** of becoming aware of them.
- A conflict of interest exists when an employee's personal interests interfere (or appear to interfere) with their professional responsibilities.
- Examples include:
  - Financial interests in a competitor, supplier, or customer.
  - Family relationships with vendors, customers, or other employees in the same reporting chain.
  - Outside employment or consulting that competes with the company's business.
- Disclosures must be made in writing to the Compliance Officer using the Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form.
- The Compliance Officer will review disclosures and determine whether mitigation measures are required.

### 2.3 Gifts and Hospitality

- Employees may not offer, give, solicit, or receive gifts or hospitality that could influence, or appear to influence, business decisions.
- **Permitted gifts:** Items of nominal value (under $50) such as branded merchandise, holiday gifts, or modest meals, provided they are infrequent and documented.
- **Reporting threshold:** Gifts from any single vendor or business partner exceeding **$100** in value must be reported to the Compliance Officer within **5 business days**.
- **Prohibited gifts:** Cash, cash equivalents (gift cards), entertainment tickets, travel, or any gift that could be perceived as a bribe.
- Gifts received in the course of business must be logged in the Gifts and Hospitality Register maintained by the Compliance team.

### 2.4 Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption

- The company prohibits bribery and corruption in all forms, including facilitation payments.
- Employees must not offer, promise, or give anything of value to government officials, customers, or business partners to influence decisions.
- The Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and UK Bribery Act apply to all company operations globally.
- Violations may result in criminal prosecution, termination, and civil liability.

## 3. Data Privacy and Protection

### 3.1 Employee Data Handling

- Employee personal data must be treated as **confidential** and handled in accordance with applicable data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.).
- Access to employee HR records is restricted to authorized personnel with a legitimate business need.
- Role-based access controls (RBAC) must be enforced for all HR information systems.
- The minimum necessary principle applies: only access the data required for your specific function.

### 3.2 Data Sharing

- Employee data must **not** be shared with unauthorized internal or external parties.
- External sharing of employee data (e.g., with vendors, government agencies, or legal counsel) requires:
  - Written approval from the HR Director or Data Protection Officer (DPO).
  - A valid legal basis or legitimate business purpose.
  - Execution of appropriate data processing agreements.
- Intra-company data sharing must comply with the company's Data Governance Framework.

### 3.3 Data Retention

- Employee HR records are retained for **7 years** after the end of employment.
- Payroll records: retained for **7 years** from the date of creation.
- Medical records: retained for the duration of employment plus **7 years**.
- Recruitment records (unsuccessful candidates): retained for **2 years** from the date of the decision.
- After the retention period expires, records must be securely destroyed using approved methods (shredding for physical records, certified data wiping for electronic records).

### 3.4 Data Breach Response

- Any suspected or confirmed data breach involving employee data must be reported to the DPO within **4 hours** of discovery.
- The DPO will coordinate the investigation, notification to affected individuals (within 72 hours where required by law), and regulatory reporting.
- Failure to report a known breach is a serious compliance violation.

## 4. Workplace Conduct

### 4.1 Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination

- The company prohibits harassment, discrimination, or bullying based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic.
- Harassment includes unwelcome conduct that creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment.
- All employees must complete **annual anti-harassment training**.
- Complaints can be filed through the Ethics Hotline, HR, or the employee's manager. All complaints are investigated promptly and confidentially.

### 4.2 Whistleblower Protection

- Employees who report suspected violations of law, regulation, or company policy in good faith are protected from retaliation.
- Retaliation includes termination, demotion, harassment, intimidation, or any other adverse employment action.
- Reports can be made through:
  - **Ethics Hotline:** 1-800-ETHICS (anonymous, 24/7)
  - **Compliance Officer:** compliance@company.com
  - **Direct Manager or HR Business Partner**
- The company will investigate all reports and take appropriate corrective action.

### 4.3 Insider Trading

- Employees who have access to material non-public information (MNPI) about the company must not trade in the company's securities or share such information with others.
- Trading windows are defined by the company's Insider Trading Policy.
- Violations may result in SEC enforcement actions, criminal prosecution, and termination.

## 5. Termination Compliance

### 5.1 Voluntary Termination

- Employees resigning must provide **2 weeks' written notice** for individual contributor roles and **4 weeks' notice** for manager and above roles.
- The notice period begins on the date the written resignation is received by the manager and HR.
- During the notice period, employees are expected to:
  - Complete a knowledge transfer to their designated successor.
  - Return all company property (laptop, badge, access cards, etc.).
  - Complete the exit checklist provided by HR.
- The company reserves the right to waive the notice period and place the employee on garden leave, with full pay and benefits through the original notice end date.

### 5.2 Involuntary Termination

- Involuntary terminations require approval from:
  - The employee's direct manager and skip-level manager.
  - HR Business Partner.
  - Legal department (for terminations involving potential legal risk).
- A termination meeting must include the manager, HR representative, and (optionally) a Legal representative.
- The employee must be provided with:
  - A written termination letter stating the effective date and reason.
  - Information about final pay, benefits continuation (COBRA), and severance (if applicable).
  - Contact information for questions.
- **Progressive discipline** must generally be followed before termination for performance reasons:
  1. Verbal warning (documented).
  2. Written warning.
  3. Performance improvement plan (PIP).
  4. Final written warning.
  5. Termination.
- Immediate termination (bypassing progressive discipline) is appropriate for gross misconduct, including theft, violence, fraud, harassment, or safety violations.

### 5.3 Exit Interviews

- Exit interviews are **mandatory** for all departing employees (voluntary and involuntary).
- The interview is conducted by HR and covers topics such as:
  - Reasons for leaving.
  - Feedback on management, team, and company culture.
  - Suggestions for improvement.
  - Benefits and final pay questions.
- Exit interview data is aggregated and reported to senior leadership quarterly to identify trends and improve retention.

### 5.4 Final Pay and Benefits

- Final paycheck must be issued within **10 business days** of the termination effective date (or sooner where required by state law).
- Final pay includes: earned wages, accrued but unused vacation (where required by law), and any outstanding expenses.
- Benefits continue through the end of the month in which termination occurs.
- COBRA election notices are sent within **5 business days** of termination.
- Employees may continue health coverage for up to **18 months** through COBRA at their own expense.

## 6. Regulatory Compliance

### 6.1 Industry-Specific Regulations

- Employees in regulated industries (finance, healthcare, government contracting) must comply with additional regulations as specified in their departmental compliance manuals.
- Required certifications must be maintained and renewed before expiration.
- Failure to maintain required certifications may result in reassignment or termination.

### 6.2 Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

- Employees involved in financial transactions must be aware of and comply with AML regulations.
- Suspicious transactions must be reported to the Compliance Officer immediately.
- The company's AML program includes customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.

### 6.3 Export Controls

- Employees involved in international operations must comply with applicable export control laws (EAR, ITAR).
- Export-controlled information, technology, or products must not be shared with non-U.S. persons without proper authorization.
- Export compliance training is mandatory for relevant roles.

## 7. Audits and Monitoring

### 7.1 Internal Audits

- Quarterly compliance audits are conducted by the Internal Audit team.
- Audit scope includes: policy adherence, data privacy, financial controls, and regulatory compliance.
- Audit findings are documented in a formal report and distributed to the relevant department heads and the Audit Committee.

### 7.2 Remediation

- Audit findings must be remediated within **30 days** for critical issues and **60 days** for non-critical issues.
- The department head is responsible for developing and implementing a remediation plan.
- The Compliance Officer tracks remediation progress and reports to the Audit Committee.

### 7.3 Non-Compliance Consequences

- Non-compliance with this policy may result in:
  - **First offense:** Written warning and mandatory retraining.
  - **Second offense:** Suspension (1-5 days) and final written warning.
  - **Third offense or severe violation:** Termination of employment.
- Non-compliance that constitutes a criminal offense may result in referral to law enforcement authorities.
- Managers who fail to enforce compliance within their teams may face disciplinary action themselves.

---

## 8. Training and Awareness

- All new employees must complete compliance onboarding within their first **30 days**.
- Annual compliance training is **mandatory** for all employees and must be completed by December 31 each year.
- Role-specific compliance training is required for:
  - Managers: Harassment prevention, data privacy, and ethics training.
  - Finance: Anti-bribery, AML, and financial controls training.
  - HR: Data privacy, employment law, and confidentiality training.
  - IT: Cybersecurity, data protection, and incident response training.
- Training completion is tracked in the Learning Management System (LMS). Non-completion by the deadline may result in account restrictions.

## 9. Reporting and Escalation

### 9.1 How to Report

- **Ethics Hotline:** 1-800-ETHICS (anonymous, available 24/7)
- **Compliance Officer:** compliance@company.com
- **HR Business Partner:** Through the HR portal or direct contact.
- **Direct Manager:** For non-sensitive compliance matters.
- **Legal Department:** legal@company.com for legal matters or potential litigation.

### 9.2 Investigation Process

- All reports are investigated promptly, thoroughly, and confidentially.
- The investigation timeline is typically **30 business days** from the date of the report.
- The investigator will interview relevant parties, review documents, and prepare a findings report.
- Corrective action, if warranted, is implemented promptly and documented.
- The reporter is notified of the investigation outcome (to the extent permitted by law and confidentiality requirements).
