# Compensation Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** June 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Total Rewards & Compensation
**Applies To:** All full-time and part-time employees

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the company's compensation philosophy, salary structure, bonus programs, equity grants, and adjustment procedures. It ensures competitive, equitable, and transparent compensation practices that attract, retain, and reward talented employees.

---

## 2. Compensation Philosophy

- The company targets compensation at the **60th-75th percentile** of the market for comparable roles.
- Compensation is determined by a combination of role, level, performance, experience, and market conditions.
- Internal equity is maintained through a structured job leveling framework and salary bands.
- Pay equity is audited annually by the Total Rewards team and an external consultant.

---

## 3. Salary Structure

### 3.1 Salary Bands

- Each job level has a defined salary band with a minimum, midpoint, and maximum.
- Salary bands are reviewed annually and adjusted based on market data.
- Current salary bands range:
  - **Level 1 (Individual Contributor):** $35,000 – $65,000
  - **Level 2 (Senior IC):** $60,000 – $95,000
  - **Level 3 (Lead/Principal):** $85,000 – $130,000
  - **Level 4 (Manager):** $90,000 – $150,000
  - **Level 5 (Senior Manager/Director):** $130,000 – $200,000
  - **Level 6 (VP):** $180,000 – $300,000
  - **Level 7 (SVP/Executive):** $250,000 – $500,000+

### 3.2 Salary Placement

- New hires are placed within the salary band based on:
  - Relevant experience and skills.
  - Internal equity with comparable roles.
  - Market data for the geographic location.
  - Budget availability.
- Offers below the band minimum require HR Director approval.
- Offers above the band midpoint require VP-level approval.
- Offers above the band maximum require CEO approval.

### 3.3 Geographic Adjustments

- Salaries are adjusted for geographic cost-of-living differences using a location-based multiplier:
  - **Tier 1 cities** (San Francisco, New York, Seattle): 1.20x base multiplier.
  - **Tier 2 cities** (Chicago, Austin, Denver): 1.10x base multiplier.
  - **Tier 3 cities** (all other US locations): 1.00x base multiplier.
  - **International locations:** Adjusted based on local market data and cost-of-living indices.
- Remote employees are compensated based on the company's location tier, not the employee's personal location, unless otherwise specified by local law.

## 4. Annual Salary Review

### 4.1 Review Cycle

- Annual salary reviews are conducted in **Q1** of each calendar year.
- Reviews are based on:
  - Performance rating from the most recent annual review.
  - Time in role and time since last adjustment.
  - Market movement and budget availability.
  - Internal equity considerations.

### 4.2 Merit Increases

- Merit increases are based on the employee's performance rating:
  - **Exceptional (5/5):** 8% – 10% increase.
  - **Exceeds Expectations (4/5):** 5% – 7% increase.
  - **Meets Expectations (3/5):** 3% – 4% increase.
  - **Needs Improvement (2/5):** 0% – 2% increase.
  - **Unsatisfactory (1/5):** 0% increase (PIP required).
- Merit increases are effective on **April 1** of each year.
- Employees must have completed at least **6 months** in their current role to be eligible for a merit increase.

### 4.3 Promotion Increases

- Promotion increases range from **10% to 20%** of the employee's current salary.
- Promotion decisions are made by the promotion committee, which includes:
  - The employee's direct manager.
  - Skip-level manager.
  - HR Business Partner.
  - Compensation analyst.
- Promotion increases are effective on the first full pay period following the promotion date.
- Promoted employees are placed within the new salary band based on their experience, performance, and internal equity.

### 4.4 Equity Adjustments

- Annual reviews include an equity analysis to identify employees whose pay has fallen below the market 50th percentile due to market movement.
- Equity adjustments (also called "market adjustments") are applied to bring these employees closer to the market median.
- Equity adjustments are separate from merit increases and are not performance-based.

## 5. Bonuses

### 5.1 Annual Performance Bonus

- Target bonus as a percentage of base salary:
  - **Individual Contributors:** 5% – 10%
  - **Managers:** 10% – 15%
  - **Directors and above:** 15% – 25%
  - **Executives:** 25% – 50%
- Actual bonus payout is determined by:
  - Individual performance rating.
  - Company performance (revenue and profitability targets).
  - Department/team goals achievement.
- Bonus payments are made in **March** of the year following the performance year.
- Employees must be employed on the bonus payment date to receive the bonus, unless otherwise specified in an employment agreement.
- Bonuses are subject to applicable taxes and withholdings.

### 5.2 Spot Awards

- Managers may nominate employees for spot awards of up to **$2,000** for exceptional contributions.
- Spot awards recognize:
  - Going above and beyond normal job responsibilities.
  - Demonstrating company values in a significant way.
  - Making a measurable impact on a project or initiative.
  - Solving a critical problem under pressure.
- Nominations require HR approval and are processed within **30 days** of approval.
- Spot awards are paid in the next regular payroll cycle and are subject to taxes.

### 5.3 Referral Bonus

- Employees who refer candidates who are successfully hired and complete **90 days** of employment receive a referral bonus of **$3,000**.
- Referral bonuses are paid 50% at hire and 50% after 90 days.
- Referral bonuses are not available for:
  - Positions already in the interview process at the time of referral.
  - Internal transfers or rehires.
  - Contract or temporary positions.
- The referring employee must disclose the referral before the candidate is interviewed.

### 5.4 Signing Bonus

- Signing bonuses may be offered to candidates in competitive roles or hard-to-fill positions.
- Typical signing bonus range: **$5,000 – $25,000** depending on role level and market conditions.
- Signing bonuses are paid in the first regular payroll cycle.
- **Clawback provision:** If the employee voluntarily resigns or is terminated for cause within **12 months** of hire, the signing bonus must be repaid in full.

### 5.5 Retention Bonus

- Retention bonuses may be offered to critical employees during organizational changes, mergers, or acquisition events.
- Retention bonus amounts and terms are determined on a case-by-case case and documented in a written agreement.
- Retention bonuses are typically paid in installments over the retention period.

## 6. Off-Cycle Adjustments

### 6.1 Types of Off-Cycle Adjustments

- **Market Adjustment:** To address competitive pressures or retention risk.
- **Promotion (Off-Cycle):** For promotions occurring outside the annual review cycle.
- **Role Change:** For significant changes in job responsibilities.
- **Error Correction:** To fix payroll or compensation errors.
- **Relocation:** To adjust for geographic moves.

### 6.2 Approval Requirements

| Adjustment Type | Approval Level |
|----------------|----------------|
| Market adjustment ≤ 5% | HR Manager |
| Market adjustment > 5% | HR Director |
| Promotion (off-cycle) | VP + HR Director |
| Role change | VP + HR Director |
| Error correction | HR Manager |
| Relocation | HR Director |

### 6.3 Retroactive Adjustments

- Retroactive adjustments are **not permitted** without Compliance sign-off.
- In limited cases (e.g., documented administrative error), retroactive adjustments may be approved by the Compliance Officer and HR Director.
- Retroactive adjustments cannot exceed **30 days** without VP approval.

## 7. Equity Compensation

### 7.1 Equity Grants

- Equity grants (stock options or restricted stock units) are determined by:
  - Role level and seniority.
  - Performance rating.
  - Market conditions and dilution targets.
  - Geographic location (for tax purposes).
- Equity grants are approved by the Board of Directors or the Compensation Committee.
- New hire equity grants are typically made on the first trading day of the month following the employee's start date.

### 7.2 Vesting Schedule

- Standard vesting: **4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff**.
  - 25% vests after 12 months of service (the cliff).
  - Remaining 75% vests monthly or quarterly over the next 36 months.
- Accelerated vesting may apply upon:
  - Change of control (double trigger): 50% acceleration.
  - Death or disability: 100% acceleration.
  - Involuntary termination without cause: Up to 12 months of accelerated vesting (at the discretion of the Compensation Committee).

### 7.3 Exercise and Expiration

- Stock options must be exercised within **90 days** of termination of employment (or sooner if the option is within 3 months of its expiration date).
- Unvested equity is forfeited upon termination, except as specified in the accelerated vesting provisions above.
- The company has a **10-year maximum term** for all stock options.

### 7.4 Equity Refresh Grants

- Annual equity refresh grants may be awarded to high-performing employees as part of the annual compensation review.
- Refresh grants are subject to the same vesting schedule as new hire grants.

---

## 8. Executive Compensation

- Executive compensation (VP and above) is determined by the Compensation Committee of the Board of Directors.
- Executive compensation includes: base salary, annual bonus, equity grants, and special benefits (e.g., executive health plan, car allowance).
- Executive compensation is benchmarked annually against a peer group of comparable companies.
- Employment agreements for executives must be reviewed by Legal and approved by the Board.

---

## 9. Pay Transparency

- The company supports pay transparency and will provide salary ranges for all open positions.
- Employees may request their own salary band information from HR at any time.
- Salary information is confidential and should not be shared with other employees, except as required by law.
- The company does not retaliate against employees who discuss compensation with each other, as protected by law.

---

## 10. Compliance and Governance

### 10.1 Pay Equity Audits

- Annual pay equity audits are conducted by the Total Rewards team and an external consultant.
- The audit analyzes pay differences by gender, race, ethnicity, and other protected characteristics, controlling for legitimate factors (role, level, experience, performance, location).
- Any unexplained pay gaps must be remediated within **60 days** of identification.

### 10.2 Compensation Committee Oversight

- The Compensation Committee of the Board of Directors oversees executive compensation and the overall compensation philosophy.
- The Committee meets quarterly and reviews:
  - Executive compensation benchmarking data.
  - Annual compensation plan results.
  - Pay equity audit findings.
  - Market trends and regulatory changes.
