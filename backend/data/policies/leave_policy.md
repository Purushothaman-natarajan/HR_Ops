# Leave Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** June 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources
**Applies To:** All full-time and part-time employees

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the guidelines, procedures, and eligibility criteria for all types of leave available to employees of the company. It ensures consistent and fair application of leave benefits across the organization while maintaining operational continuity.

---

## 2. Annual Leave (Vacation)

### 2.1 Accrual

- Full-time employees accrue **2.5 days of annual leave per month** (30 days per calendar year).
- Part-time employees accrue leave on a pro-rata basis calculated as: `(hours worked per week / 40) × 2.5 days per month`.
- Accrual begins on the employee's first day of employment. New hires may use accrued leave after completing a 90-day introductory period.
- Accrual is calculated on the last day of each calendar month and credited on the first business day of the following month.

### 2.2 Carry Forward

- Unused annual leave may be carried forward to the next calendar year, up to a maximum of **15 days**.
- Any unused days beyond 15 will be forfeited on December 31 of the accrual year. The company does not pay out forfeited annual leave.
- Employees are encouraged to use their leave within the accrual year. Managers should proactively discuss leave balances with their direct reports during Q4.

### 2.3 Request Procedure

- Leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least **5 working days** in advance.
- Requests are approved by the employee's direct manager. The manager must respond within 2 business days.
- If the manager is unavailable, the request should be escalated to the next-level manager.
- Leave requests during blackout periods (see Section 2.4) require **VP-level approval**.

### 2.4 Blackout Periods

- The following periods are designated as blackout periods where annual leave is restricted:
  - **Q4 Close (October 15 – December 31):** Maximum 2 team members may be on leave simultaneously per department.
  - **Year-End Close (January 1 – January 31):** Restricted to critical business needs only.
  - **Audit Periods:** As designated by the Finance department.
- Exceptions may be granted for documented emergencies or pre-approved sabbaticals.

### 2.5 Carry-Over Limitations

- Employees returning from extended leave (parental, medical, sabbatical) may carry forward up to **20 days** in the year of return, with HR approval.
- Leave balances exceeding the carry-forward limit must be used before the end of Q1 of the following year.

## 3. Sick Leave

### 3.1 Entitlement

- Full-time employees receive **12 days of fully paid sick leave** per calendar year.
- Part-time employees receive sick leave on a pro-rata basis: `(hours worked per week / 40) × 12 days per year`.
- Sick leave is granted at the beginning of each calendar year and does not accumulate beyond the annual entitlement.

### 3.2 Unused Sick Leave

- Unused sick leave does **not** carry forward to the next calendar year.
- Sick leave is not paid out upon termination of employment.

### 3.3 Medical Certificate Requirements

- A medical certificate from a licensed healthcare provider is **required** for:
  - Any absence exceeding **3 consecutive business days**.
  - The first day of absence if the employee has used more than 5 sick days in the current quarter.
  - Recurring patterns of Monday/Friday absences (as determined by HR).
- The certificate must include: the employee's name, date of examination, diagnosis (if the employee consents), and recommended duration of absence.
- Medical certificates must be submitted within **2 business days** of the employee's return to work.

### 3.4 Sick Leave for Family Members

- Employees may use up to **5 days** of their sick leave to care for an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling) with a serious health condition.
- Documentation similar to personal sick leave requirements applies.

### 3.5 Abuse of Sick Leave

- Patterns of sick leave usage that suggest abuse (e.g., frequent absences adjacent to weekends or holidays) may trigger an HR investigation.
- Disciplinary action for confirmed abuse may include written warning, suspension, or termination.

## 4. Parental Leave

### 4.1 Maternity Leave

- Eligible employees receive **26 weeks of maternity leave at full pay**.
- Eligibility: Employees with at least **12 months of continuous service** prior to the expected date of delivery.
- Leave may begin up to **4 weeks** before the expected delivery date or on the date of delivery, whichever is earlier.
- The employee must provide at least **30 days' notice** before the expected start date, or as soon as practicable in cases of unexpected delivery.
- Remaining annual leave may be used immediately before or after maternity leave, subject to manager approval.

### 4.2 Paternity Leave

- Eligible employees receive **4 weeks of paternity leave at full pay**.
- Eligibility: Employees with at least **6 months of continuous service**.
- Leave must be taken within **12 weeks** of the child's birth or adoption placement.
- Can be taken as a continuous block or split into two separate periods of at least 1 week each, with manager approval.

### 4.3 Adoption Leave

- Eligible employees receive **12 weeks of adoption leave at full pay**.
- Eligibility: Employees with at least **12 months of continuous service**.
- Leave may begin upon placement of the child or up to **2 weeks before** the expected placement date.
- Applies to both domestic and international adoptions. International adoption documentation requirements may vary.

### 4.4 Extended Parental Leave (Unpaid)

- After the paid parental leave period, employees may take an additional **12 weeks of unpaid parental leave**.
- This extended leave is available for maternity, paternity, or adoption leave.
- The employee's position is guaranteed for the first 8 weeks of unpaid leave. Beyond 8 weeks, the company will make reasonable efforts to restore the employee to an equivalent position.
- Benefits continue during the first 8 weeks of unpaid leave. The employee may elect to continue benefits beyond 8 weeks at their own cost.

### 4.5 Return to Work

- Employees returning from parental leave are entitled to return to their previous position or an equivalent role with no reduction in pay, benefits, or seniority.
- A phased return-to-work arrangement may be negotiated, reducing hours gradually over a period of up to 4 weeks.
- Employees must provide at least **5 business days' notice** of their intended return date.

---

## 5. Unpaid Leave

### 5.1 Eligibility and Duration

- Employees may request up to **30 days of unpaid leave** per calendar year.
- Unpaid leave is available after all paid leave entitlements (annual, sick) have been exhausted.
- Requests must be submitted at least **15 business days** in advance with a written justification.

### 5.2 Approval Process

- Unpaid leave requires approval from both the direct manager and the HR department.
- Requests exceeding 10 consecutive days require additional **VP-level approval**.
- The company reserves the right to deny unpaid leave requests based on business needs.

### 5.3 Impact on Benefits

- Unpaid leave of **5 or fewer consecutive business days** has no impact on benefits or tenure calculations.
- Unpaid leave exceeding 5 consecutive business days:
  - Health insurance premiums: The employee must continue to pay their share. The company will continue to pay its share for the first 30 days.
  - Retirement contributions: Suspended during the unpaid period. Service credit is not accrued.
  - Seniority: Continues to accrue for the first 15 days. Beyond 15 days, seniority is frozen until the employee returns.

---

## 6. Bereavement Leave

- **Immediate family** (spouse, child, parent, sibling): **5 days** of paid leave.
- **Extended family** (grandparent, grandchild, in-law, domestic partner): **3 days** of paid leave.
- **Additional unpaid leave** of up to 5 days may be granted with manager approval.
- Documentation (death certificate or obituary) must be provided within 5 business days of return.

---

## 7. Jury Duty and Civic Leave

- Employees summoned for jury duty or court attendance receive **paid leave** for the duration of the service, up to **10 business days** per calendar year.
- Beyond 10 days, leave is unpaid but the employee's position is guaranteed.
- Employees must notify their manager as soon as they receive a summons.

---

## 8. Voting Leave

- Employees may take up to **2 hours of paid leave** on Election Day to vote, if polls are not open during non-working hours.
- Employees must notify their manager at least **3 business days** in advance.

---

## 9. Sabbatical Leave

- Employees with **5 or more years** of continuous service are eligible for a **4-week paid sabbatical**.
- Sabbaticals may be used for personal development, travel, volunteer work, or rest.
- Requests must be submitted at least **6 months** in advance and approved by the employee's VP and HR.
- A sabbatical may be taken once every 5 years of service.
- The employee's position is not guaranteed during sabbatical, but the company will make reasonable efforts to restore the employee to an equivalent role.

---

## 10. Leave Interaction Rules

- **Leave stacking:** Employees may not take more than 5 consecutive weeks of leave (combining any leave types) without HR approval.
- **Notice of return:** For all extended leaves (>10 business days), employees must provide at least 5 business days' notice before returning.
- **Overlapping leave:** If an employee's approved leave coincides with a company-mandated work event (e.g., annual meeting, training), the employee is expected to attend unless the leave is for medical reasons.

---

## 11. Manager Responsibilities

- Managers must respond to leave requests within **2 business days**.
- Managers must ensure adequate coverage during employee absences.
- Managers must not discourage employees from taking legitimate leave.
- Managers must maintain confidentiality regarding medical and parental leave.
- Managers must report extended absences (>5 days) to HR for tracking purposes.

---

## 12. Violations and Disciplinary Action

- Failure to follow leave request procedures (e.g., not submitting requests in advance, not providing documentation) may result in the leave being classified as unauthorized.
- Unauthorized absence of 3 or more consecutive days may be considered job abandonment and grounds for termination.
- Falsifying medical certificates or other leave documentation is grounds for immediate termination.
- Repeated misuse of leave policies may result in progressive discipline, up to and including termination.
